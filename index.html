New York Times Headlines 1990-2020

Welcome to Our Project

Our world is reflected and influenced by the reporting of major media companies, which observe and spread news stories about events in a digestible and meaningful way. The tecnological advancements of the last several decades have also shown us that media is not only a game of reporting, but also capturing readers' attention. Thus, headlines of news articles seek to toe the line between delivering information with journalistic integrity and also piquing peoples' curiousity. We sought to answer the following questions about New York Times Headlines from the years 1990 to 2020.

1. What was important to readers over the years 1990-2020 How is it different to what is important to readers now?

2. How can we piece together historical events and stories through the headlines of the New York Times?

Through the years

1991

In 1991, the Soviet Union officially dissolved amidst great political instability and calls for reform in its eastern satellite states. Mikhail Gorbachev, the former president of the Soviet Union, attempted to improve government transparency and civil liberties like freedom of speech and press through his policies of Glasnost. He even attempted introducing market reforms to the Soviet economy through his policies of Perestroika.

What was the New York Times' coverage of these reforms?

In the 1991 opinion section article (author not found in our dataset), titled "Broken Glasnost", heavily criticized the Glasnost policies as a failing to lead up to their ideological promises of openness, "Now, instead of openness, Orwellian propaganda spews forth". Similar sentiments are echoed in George Whitney's 1991 article "Moscow's Iron Hand: Gorbachev, Facing Growing Disorder, Chooses Control over Liberalization".

In the 11 thousand headlines that we have ChatGPT's y-values for, 32 headlines contain the words "soviet", "russian", or "ussr". The average catestrophe-to-miracle sentiment score of those 32 headlines was around -0.06. This is slightly lower than America's score of approximately -0.01.

Amidst great political and economic instability The Russian oil industry suffered heavy hits. Russian involvement in the Gulf War also stretched its resources thin. That same year, the 1991 Racha 7.2 Magnitude Earthquake struck the region of modern day Georgia, killing around 100 civilians. All of these events contributed to the fall of the Soviet Union, and the New York Times covered all these events closely. The word 'Soviet' is included 566 times in our database in the year 1991, one of the most commonly occuring keywords that year.

When we search the top keywords in the headlines of most years, the name of the president at the time usually shows up. Like Clinton and Bush. 2016 marked a big change in this trend. In our analysis of the headlines, in the 2016 election, the names of multiple candidates and the president at the time all show up as top keywords in the headlines.
